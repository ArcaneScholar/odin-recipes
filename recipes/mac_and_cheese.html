Mac and Cheese

Mac and cheese is the definition of creamy comfort. Elbow pasta is cooked until tender, then folded into a smooth cheese sauce made with butter, milk, and a generous amount of melted cheddar. The sauce clings to every piece, turning a simple dish into something rich and satisfying. Baked with a golden breadcrumb crust or served straight from the pot, mac and cheese is a classic that never fails to bring warmth to the table.

Ingredients

  • 1 (8 ounce) box elbow macaroni
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • ground black pepper to taste
  • 2 cups milk
  • 2 cups shredded Cheddar cheese

Directions

  1. Gather the ingredients.
  2. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Cook elbow macaroni in the boiling water, stirring occasionally until cooked through but firm to the bite, 8 minutes. Elbow macaroni cooking in boiling water
  3. At the same time, melt butter in a saucepan over medium heat. butter melting in a sauce pan
  4. Add flour, salt, and pepper and stir until smooth, about 5 minutes. butter and flour being whisked in a saucepan
  5. Pour in milk slowly, while stirring continuously. Continue to cook and stir until mixture is smooth and bubbling, about 5 minutes, making sure the milk doesn't burn. milk being added slowly to butter and flour mixture
  6. Add Cheddar cheese and stir until melted, 2 to 4 minutes. Cheese being whisked into roux
  7. Drain macaroni and fold into cheese sauce until coated. Folding pasta into cheese sauce
  8. Serve hot and enjoy!
